It is a truth universally acknowledged that most films with an agenda are ham-fisted; propaganda is anathema to subtlety. This isnt to say that propaganda is necessarily dreary or that it cant be artistic- early Soviet artists produced works that are so cool they are packaged, sold, and happily purchased by capitalists. But most efforts fall far short of Battleship Potemkin (1925).

All of this to say that Toilet: A Love Story (2017), while an occasionally superficially fun film, has the subtlety of an oncoming freight train and the artistic merit of the 2020 socially-distanced Democratic National Convention. It also comes with all the baggage of a Volkswagen in 1945.
