On first reflection it seems superfluous to watch horror right now. Reality, but make it scarier? It seems like the perfect time for Bridgerton, or White Collar, or Parks & Recreation. For some of us, Magic Mike: XXL has found its way into heavy rotation. No wrong answers here.

Im not a connoisseur of the genre as a whole. My anxiety keeps me on the edge of my seat without any additional stimulous required, thanks muchly, but I do enjoy the gothic, the macabre, Victoriana, Expressionism, and a good cry, so many offerings in the genre are well within my wheelhouse.

the umbrella is large enough for both Rebecca to Psycho. Moreover, to my mind horror is interesting not because it is frightening but because it is so well suited to grapple with the most profound questions of existence. In what other genre are the questions of good or evil or the afterlife more central? What have we earned, what do we owe one another, and can we reconcile that with what we experience? What truly frightens us?

It is perhaps because of this potential intesity of feeling that horror can give itself over to hyperbole, and to hyperbolic beauty.
