I was inspired to write this post by a joke to a friend that, "I live as much of my life like a Bollywood film as possible!" Largely because I forget I am singling out loud and catch myself mid "aankhon main teri" in public; it occurred to me that a post would be a fun opportunity to point to some of the fantastic and life-enhancing accessories available to fans of Indian cinema.
I am also tired of running across websites recommending ways to throw an "Indian party". As far as I can tell, the secret is to buy some bright-colored fabric and create an indoor orientalist -inspired harem a la Eugène Delacroix. There is a weird-smelling store in nearly every town offering questionably-sourced imports allowing people everything they need to create an orientalist harem in their own home. Even Vogue got in on the act. But it is well past time to move beyond patchouli.
The Art
Until fairly recently, movie posters in India were hand-painted works of art. Thanks to the dedication of a few individuals and the magic of the internet, these pop-art masterpieces are now available to the discerning public. For original art, they are a complete steal at $30-$300; a Warhol poster of Liz Taylor recently sold for $92,000. You can buy vintage Bollywood posters for $50 and less. CNN did a fascinating report about the work being done by the owners of Indian Hippy to preserve hand-drawn posters. It is well worth watching. You can even have yourself painted into your favorite film!
There is obviously more to the South Asian art scene than Bollywood; India is home to a buzzing contemporary art scene, as highlighted in Khan's cinematic Dhobi Ghat (2010). The Guggenheim in NY recently hosted a retrospective of V.S. Gaitonde's work. Similarly, the V & A recently hosted a special exhibition of M.F. Husain's final nine paintings. Vogue India recently conducted a interview with the show's currator Sandhini Poddar, which provides a good entrance-point to learning more about some of India contemporary and modern artists.

Fair-trade stores are also all over the place online. The 10,000 Villages website features a lot of lovely fair-trade sourced jewelry from South Asia. If you are feeling flush, Vogue India featured the jewelry appearing in Project Blossoming, the proceeds of which will support Project Nanhi Kali, an organization seeking to promote educational opportunities for girl children in India. If you are not feeling flush, you can still enjoy looking at the pretty things, watch the video, and learn more about women helping women in India.

Bangles, glass or metal, bring jingle-jangle music to any outfit, and I carefully coordinated them with my outfits in India in a doomed attempt to bring order from chaos. Glass bangles are often made by children so be careful and aware about where you buy them.

The Mhendi
Mhendi, also called henna, is a lot of fun. It is also popular with the hippy-dippy set. Whadayado. I dont know as much about the history of mhendi as I would like, but I do know that it is used throughout the Near East, North Africa, into Central Asia, and in South Asia, although its use never made it as far east as Bengal- perhaps mhendi doesnt grow there. In Lucknow at least, the designs as worn in Umrao Jaan (1981) (specifically colored fingertips and palms) were favored for brides; the intricate patterns now famous in the west were more common in Rajasthan and other parts of western South Asia. The patterns favored in other parts of the world are different again. Although mhendi is a part of many South Asian wedding ceremonies, while I was living in Naukuchiatal we applied mhendi as a recreational activity- it is a lovely way to build relationships between women, one which requires time and trust. I have also heard that in some places, the name of the groom is written in mhendi on the bride's body. Certainly mhendi, as well as long rows of red and white bangles, are a way to identify a new bride; wearing mhendi and glass bangles to the elbow make housework more difficult (no one wants to wear off their mhendi doing dishes), and the tradition can allow a new bride some breathing room in a new household.  
The Beauty Products
Everyone I knew while studying in Lucknow with AIIS, including myself, used Himalaya kajal - it was simply the best on the market.

I found the plethora of British products still available on the market fascinating, most especially Yardley's and Ponds, from which lines you could buy soaps, talcum powders, scents, and creams in every large store in every city I visited. Ponds talcum powder (sandalwood) was particularly lovely for the summer. Wearing talc made me feel like a black-and-white picture co-starring Cary Grant; also, other deodorants melted strangely in the heat. Turns out Grandma was absolutely on to something.
Gwen Stephani wore bindi for a while in the 90s. She also dressed Asian women like dolls. Dont do it. Cochella, you deeply questionable annual fashion moment, I am looking at you. Note: a bindi is not the same as a tika; the first is a symbol of married status and/or an accessory, and the second is part of a blessing. In Naukuchiatal, where Shiva was favored in the public sphere, tikas usually included rice, turmeric, and a red powder that may or may not have been abir. Different sects favor different styles of tikas, but in Naukuchiatal they were applied with a thumb starting between the eyes and drawing upwards, followed by a few grains of uncooked rice.
The Food!
the cornerstone of any nutritious breakfast
The internet is full of recipes for different different Indian foods. I dont know much about any of them, so you will have to find the websites yourself. I will, however, recommend The Calcutta Kitchen cookbook by Udit Sharkhel. I haven’t tried any of the recipes yet (no excuses; I own the book), but the photos are beautiful, so you can always pop them out of the book and hang them on the walls of your kitchen.
Or eat we did at AIIS Kolkata- brew a pot of tea, add milk, and eat cucumber and tomatoe sandwiches with fresh butter, and Lay’s potatoe chips on the side. Throw in some McVities Digestive Biscuits and full fat yogurt, put on a Satyaji Ray film, and call it a party. “Authentic” and “inauthentic” foods are impossible options- something is either food or it isn’t, people eat it in Indian or they don’t. And with 1.24 billion citizens, someone probably does.    
The Drinks!
There was also chai. It was that awesome. 
Chai is a must. My first experience in India was up in Uttarakhand where chai masala is popular, which is to say chai with cardamom and some nutmeg, and whatever else the gentlemen at the chai stands and restaurants tossed in. I'm not 100% sure. But it was delicious.  
If you are feeling neo-colonial, a gin and tonic is the traditional choice, although as I say, it is more of a neo-colonial thing than a popular beverage option, particularly since good Hindus, good Muslims, and good women are not, for various reasons, supposed to drink. People drink anyway, but certainly not everyone. Alcoholics included for comic effect in early Hindi-language films were nearly always Christians, vamps, murderers, traitors, or members of the LGBT community. That will have to wait for another blog post.

The Adventure!
Studying in India is the best way I have found to get people to pay for me to have adventures in India. It is not the only way, but it is a winning option. There are about a million reasons to study abroad, and any google search of "why study abroad" will take you to lists, discussions, and arguments in favor. I studied in India three times for a total of 14 and a half months on the Subcontinent. I am in favor.


Here are some ways to do it:
The Gilman Scholarship (undergraduate)
The Critical Language Scholarship (CLS) (undergraduate and graduate student) 
American Institute of Indian Studies (AIIS) (undergraduate and graduate students)
Foreign Language and Area Studies (FLAS) (undergraduate? and graduate students)- these are distributed by individual universities, just like the flagship language programs 
Your school, college, university, or community may offer additional opportunities. Poke around and find out. The internet is for more than just cat videos.
