In celebration of 100 years of film-making in South Asia (what many consider India's first film, Raja Harishchandra, having been released in 1913) the internet was filled with "Top 10" lists, all of them including Sholay (1975) and Mughal-e-Azim (1960). I have decided to take a more meta route and share with you my Top 10 List of Lists. More accurately, my top-10 destinations for information about Hindi cinema.

Manas

A UCLA project highlighting different facets of South Asian arts and culture, this site has some interesting biographies, film summaries, and analysis, primarily of Hindi/Urdu cinema, but not exclusively. The opening pages featuring general history and background of are a good place to start learning more about cinema in India.

Beth Loves Bollywood

Self explanatory, except that Beth loves the Bangla-language cinema of Tollywood as much as anything else, which is fantastic. It is not easy to find good reviews of Bangla-language films in English. Her blog is an entertaining and entirely worthwhile read.

Ugly,Ugly, Bollywood Fugly

Another of Beth's projects, this blog showcases the worst costume choices ever made in Bollywood, Tollywood, and Kollywood. This is a blog I never knew the internet always needed. I had never stopped to really consider just how gloriously awful some of these costumes are. Now we all can.

The Guardian Film Blog

This is a reservoir of thoughtful, articulate reflections on Hindi-language cinema, the role of cinema in globalization, and the cultural significance of Hindi-language cinema in the UK's South Asian diaspora communities. I like to dip into this site when in need of inspiration or insight.

Mr. and Mrs. 55

Any blogger who takes the time to seriously reflect on the exuberant hairstyles of 1970s cinema is probably someone I would want to meet. My favorite post is a pictorial overview of the work of costume designer Mani Rabadi, who gave the world some unforgettable sartorial statements.

Vogue India 

For coverage of South Asian and global cultural happenings, fashion trends, current events, who's who, and of course cinema, told with sophistication and elan, look first to Vogue India. Look no further.

The Aerogram

Exactly what is says on the label: A curated take on South Asian art, literature, life and news. The voices found on The Aerogram are generally breezy and opinionated, and easily digestible. Amusement and thinking ensues.

Buzzfeed

Specifically contributing editors Imaan Sheikh and Rega Jha. If you haven't yet read Sheikh's "12 Bollywood Pokemon Cards That You Absolutely Must Collect" you are probably doing the internet wrong.

philip'sfil-ums

Despite the punny name choice, Dr. Philip Lutgendorf's blog, which is linked to his course offerings at the University of Iowa, is far more intellectual than mine. For that I resent his blog a little bit, but it is worth reading nevertheless.

Shemaroo Movies

This is actually a youtube chanel, but it is a great place to find high-quality, legal (ie not pirated) prints of old films for free! This is a particularly good option because, as columnist Jon Brooks so aptly noted, Netflix is not always the place to go for the esoteric and interesting. This is particularly true of Netflix streaming, which is mostly crap, Veer-Zaara notwithstanding. Bandini (1963) is where I recommend beginning.
